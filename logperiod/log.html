Cycles of the logistic map

Introduction

The logistic map is defined as:

xk+1 = f (xk) ≡ r xk (1 − xk).

An n-cycle means

x n+1 = x1.

We ask at what r values, an n-cycle is possible and stable.

We first transform the logistic map to an algebraically simpler form (as suggested by Saha and Strogatz) by a change of variables

x ← r (x − 1/2), R ≡ (r2 − 2 r) / 4.

to

xn+1 = R − xn2.

For later convenience, we further define T = R/4, and

d ≡ (−2)n x1 x2 ... xn

The cycle starts at d = 1, and becomes unstable at d = −1.
